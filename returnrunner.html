Return Runner Case Study

Product Summary

Return Runner is a convenience app that allows users to process returns without ever leaving their house. During our research, we discovered that one of the biggest pain points with managing one's returns is having to go to the store to do them.

The Goal

Our goal is to reduce the hassle of having to return an item you purchased online to a physical location by providing a service that picks it up at your doorstep.

User Research

In this project we begun by brainstorming potential solutions to real life pain points. Returning items to the store is one for many. We interviewed people to get more insight into shopping habits and confirmed our hypothesis to be true. We focused on learning more about our potential users to design an application for a service that outsourced returns. During user research we discovered most people shop online and quite often need to make returns. We also learned that people liked to have multiple options for making returns and a seamless transparent experience.

Potential Features

In this phase of the project we brainstormed and thought outside of the box at potential features for our application based on research findings. We then prioritized our potential features.

Story Boards


We created a storyboard to further empathize with our users.

User Journey

We then focused on using the user journey to help us pinpoint opportunities to improve our product.

Wireframe Sketches

We used the information learned to sketch our 1st iteration of our product design.

Usability Test & Findings

We created a user test plan an conducted three user tests in wich we instructed each user to create and account, start a return and track a return. We conducted user test on wireframes. Our findings from the test were as follow:

  • Users were confused about what was a button and what was a picture
  • Users were unsure what the input fields were asking them to do
  • Users thought the sign up process was too long and complicated
  • Users had a hard time figuring out where to start a return

    • Improvements

    Our testing result findings led us to create and iterate on our wireframes. We made changed to the sign up. Simplified user experience. And offered more clarity with input fields. Our full workflow and prototype allowed users to start a return solicit service and track their item until return was complete.

    In Closing

    Final Thoughts: During our testing we found that the “notifications on/off screen” and the “track your return” screen were too simple. We added informational details to the prototype in order to give the user more of a confirmation. Those details were price, location, date, and progress. We also found that users were confused about what was a button and what was a picture and thought the sign up process was too long. Besides a few minor fixes we needed to make, the rest of the app worked pretty well and we are happy with its new iterations.

    Future Opportunities: We will need to design another interface for the drivers who will be picking up the packages for Return Runner. We also would need to install a pretty hefty navigation capability so that we are able to map drivers to the correct locations to drop off the returns. All together the design process was challenging and fun and we are happy with the results.
